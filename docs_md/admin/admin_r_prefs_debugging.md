## Preferences for the formula inspector

Path to the preferences:

Menu Window > Preferences

Dialog Preferences > Formulas > Formula Inspector

In the default setting, the branches of a formula are also calculated and represented in the Formula Inspector that are not relevant to the determination of the result. Results of non-relevant branches are represented in gray.

To suppress the evaluation in the formula inspector, the check box Enable inspection of non-evaluated conditional branches in the formula inspector must be deselected. Branches whose evaluation are not relevant to the result will then be marked as Not evaluated!.

Enter a Maximum number of drop-down levels so that only a limited number of terms can be opened in the formula inspector.

The default value is 20.

To avoid restricting the drop-down levels, enter a value of 0. In the case of very extensive formulas, this setting can result in longer waiting times.
